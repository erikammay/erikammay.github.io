Alternative Vehicle Fuels

Vehicles are essential to some people in their everyday life, but they cause a lot of pollution. Every day, millions of barrels of oil are consumed. 57% is from transportation alone. We need to start using alternative fuels such as solar power, electricity, biofuel, compressed air, methanol, and hydrocarbon.


Out of the millions of barrels of oil consumed a day, 57% of it is used for transportation. The USA, China, and India have the highest number of registered vehicles with 254 million, 233 million, 147 million. They are also, uncoincidentally, the top three countries with the highest CO2 emissions. All these vehicles used daily are extremely oil intensive and bad for the environment.


There are many alternative fuels to stop damaging the environment such as solar power, electricity, biofuel, compressed air, methanol, and hydrocarbon. Solar power is pollution free, quiet, renewable, and energy efficient. Electricity is pollution free and cleaner than gas powered cars. Biofuel is biodegradable and economical. Compressed air has cold air emissions only, so there isn’t unnecessary heat. Methanol reduces emission, and hydrocarbon is pollution free and renewable. Teslas use electricity instead of gas, but unfortunately, it is not affordable for most people. Hybrids, on the other hand, use both electricity and gas, so it doesn’t have as much gas as the average car, and it is somewhat affordable. More companies should make affordable electric cars.


Tons of oil is used every day all over the world. 57% of consumption is from transportation. We need to use alternative fuels, and companies should make affordable environmentally friendly cars.
